📚 Key Vocabulary: Core AI Ethics Terms

algorithm
/ˈælɡərɪðəm/
A set of rules or instructions that a computer follows
The recruitment algorithm analyzed thousands of applications.
bias
/ˈbaɪəs/
Unfair preference or prejudice; systematic errors in AI
The system showed bias against darker skin tones.
accountability
/əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Being responsible and expected to explain decisions
We need clear accountability when AI makes mistakes.
autonomous
/ɔːˈtɑːnəməs/
Able to operate independently without human control
Autonomous vehicles can drive themselves.

📚 Key Vocabulary: Values & Discussion

justice
/ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Fair treatment and equality
We demand justice for those harmed by biased AI.
privacy
/ˈpraɪvəsi/
Freedom from having your personal information seen
Smart home devices raise privacy questions.
ethical dilemma
/ˈeθɪkəl dɪˈlemə/
Choosing between options, neither clearly right or wrong
Should the car save the passenger or pedestrian?
stakeholder
/ˈsteɪkhoʊldər/
A person or group with an interest in something
Patients and doctors are stakeholders in healthcare AI.

🎯 Activity 1: AI Ethical Dilemmas

📝 Instructions:

  1. Work in small groups (3-4 students)
  2. Each group receives a different cue card
  3. Read the scenario carefully
  4. Discuss the discussion prompts (15 minutes)
  5. Present your conclusions to the class

Grammar Focus: Second Conditional

If + past simple, would/could/might + base verb

✓ If I were the CEO, I would shut down the system.
✓ If the AI had to choose, it might prioritize safety.
✓ If we implemented this, we could reduce bias.

📋 Cue Card 1: Biased Recruitment

The Scenario:

A major tech company has developed an AI recruitment system. After one year, data shows the AI consistently ranks male candidates higher than equally qualified female candidates for technical positions. The AI learned this bias from historical hiring data.

Discussion Prompts:

  • What would you do if you were the CEO?
  • Who is responsible for this bias?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of continuing versus shutting down?
Key Vocabulary: bias, accountability, discrimination, deploy, stakeholder

📋 Cue Card 2: Autonomous Vehicle

The Scenario:

An autonomous vehicle is traveling at high speed when it detects an unavoidable accident. The AI must choose: swerve left and potentially hit a pedestrian, OR continue straight and crash into a barrier, likely injuring the passenger. It has 0.3 seconds to decide.

Discussion Prompts:

  • If you were programming the vehicle, what would you prioritize?
  • Should passengers know in advance how their vehicle would respond?
  • What if all manufacturers programmed vehicles differently?
Key Vocabulary: autonomous, ethical dilemma, consequence, accountability

📋 Cue Card 3: Healthcare Resources

The Scenario:

A hospital uses AI to prioritize patients for organ transplants and ICU beds. The algorithm analyzes survival rates and recovery likelihood. Critics argue it disadvantages elderly patients and those with pre-existing conditions. Supporters claim it maximizes lives saved.

Discussion Prompts:

  • Would you continue using this AI system as a hospital administrator?
  • How would you balance efficiency with fairness?
  • What if your own family member was ranked lower?
Key Vocabulary: justice, equality, discrimination, stakeholder, consequence

🎧 Activity 2: Listening Comprehension

📝 Instructions:

  1. You will listen to an audio/video clip about AI ethics
  2. First listening: General comprehension (don't write)
  3. Second listening: Complete the chart with specific details
  4. Compare answers with a partner
  5. Class discussion of findings

What to Listen For:

  • What is the ethical problem?
  • Who are the stakeholders affected?
  • What solutions are proposed?
  • What key terminology is used?
  • What are the main arguments and counter-arguments?

💭 Activity 4: Opinion Statements

📝 Instructions:

  1. Read statements about AI bias and accountability
  2. Choose your position on each statement
  3. Find classmates with different opinions
  4. Discuss and justify your viewpoints
  5. Use second conditional structures in your arguments

Sample Statement:

"When an AI system makes a discriminatory decision, the person most accountable should be the programmer who wrote the code."

Your Response:
  • Do you agree or disagree?
  • Why? Give reasons and examples
  • What would happen if...? (use second conditional)

⚖️ Activity 5: Agree/Disagree Scale

📝 Instructions:

  1. Rate each statement on a scale of 1-5
  2. 1 = Disagree totally | 5 = Agree totally
  3. Stand in different corners of the room based on your rating
  4. Discuss your position with nearby students
  5. One person from each group presents their reasoning

Sample Statement:

"The benefits of AI-powered personalization are worth giving up some privacy."

1 - Disagree 3 - Neutral 5 - Agree

🗣️ Activity 6: Two-Minute Debates

📝 Instructions:

  1. Work in pairs - one FOR, one AGAINST the motion
  2. Prepare your arguments (5 minutes)
  3. Each person speaks for 2 minutes
  4. No interruptions during speaking time
  5. Class votes on the most convincing argument

Useful Debate Language:

  • "I would like to argue that..."
  • "The key issue here is..."
  • "I respectfully disagree with..."
  • "On the contrary..."
  • "The evidence suggests..."
  • "This clearly demonstrates..."

🗣️ Motion 1: Political Campaigning

"AI should be banned in political campaigning"

✓ Arguments FOR

  • Manipulation through micro-targeting
  • Spread of misinformation/deepfakes
  • Undermines democracy
  • Unfair advantage for wealthy

✗ Arguments AGAINST

  • Restricts free speech
  • Can educate voters efficiently
  • Traditional methods also manipulate
  • Technology isn't the problem

🗣️ Motion 2: Facial Recognition

"Facial recognition should be used in all public spaces"

✓ Arguments FOR

  • Deters crime effectively
  • Finds missing persons quickly
  • Makes society safer overall
  • We already have CCTV cameras

✗ Arguments AGAINST

  • Mass surveillance violates privacy
  • Risk of misidentification
  • Can be abused by governments
  • Affects minorities disproportionately

🏛️ Activity 7: Citizens' Forum

📝 Instructions:

  1. Each student receives a role card
  2. Read your role carefully and prepare (5 minutes)
  3. The Councillor (moderator) opens the meeting
  4. Each stakeholder presents their problem (2 minutes each)
  5. Group discussion and solution proposals (15 minutes)
  6. Councillor summarizes and closes the meeting

Forum Topic:

"Bias and Accountability in City AI Systems"

🎭 Citizens' Forum Roles

👨‍⚖️ Local Councillor (Moderator)

Your task: Chair the meeting professionally, ensure everyone contributes, summarize solutions

👮 Police Officer

Your concern: Predictive policing AI creates community tension and has high error rates

🏥 Healthcare Administrator

Your concern: AI diagnosis contradicts doctors; insurance AI denies coverage unfairly

👨‍🏫 Teacher

Your concern: AI grading favors certain styles; tracking algorithms limit student opportunities

🛡️ Privacy Advocate

Your concern: Traffic system collects data without consent; no appeals process for AI decisions

💼 Business Owner

Your concern: Regulations too expensive; public distrust hurting legitimate AI applications

💬 Functional Language for Forums

Stating Problems:

  • From a [perspective]...
  • The problem we're encountering is...
  • We've noticed that...
  • This poses a serious problem because...

Proposing Solutions:

  • I would suggest that...
  • One possible solution would be...
  • Perhaps we could...
  • We should consider...

Moderator Language:

  • Let me summarize what we've discussed...
  • We need to address...
  • Let's hear from [person]...
  • To reach a consensus, we should...

🔧 Activity 8: AI Tool Problems

📝 Instructions:

  1. Work in pairs or small groups
  2. Each group receives a common AI tool problem
  3. Brainstorm practical solutions (5 minutes)
  4. Share your solutions with another group
  5. Class discussion: Which solutions work best?

Example Problems:

  • The AI gives vague answers that don't help my situation
  • The AI provides "facts" that aren't true (hallucinations)
  • The AI's writing is too formal/informal
  • The AI doesn't remember context from earlier conversation
  • The AI refuses to help with something I think is reasonable
